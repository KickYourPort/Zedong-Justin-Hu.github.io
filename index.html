Research Projects

Flexible Oxide-oxide Vertically Aligned Nanocomposite (VAN) Memristor for Flexible Neuromorphic Computing

  • Proposed and fabricated a novel flexible memristor using oxide-oxide VAN thin films via pulsed laser deposition (PLD), e-beam lithography (EBL), reactive ion etching (RIE), and sputtering.
  • Optimized interface density to enhance device performance, reducing set/reset voltage by 60%, and improving endurance (>1012 cycles), retention (>105 seconds), and device-to-device variation (μ = 1.5 V, σ = 0.1 V, σ/μ = 0.07).
  • Conducted microstructural analysis (XRD, TEM, AFM) and electrical testing with a probe station and Keithley 4200A. Collaborated with University of Texas at Arlington for COMSOL-based switching mechanism analysis.
  • Integrated the device into a spiking neural network (SNN) using Python and MATLAB, achieving >95% accuracy on the MNIST dataset.
  • Supervised two undergraduates in electrical characterization techniques.

Metal-oxide VAN Bilayer Memristor for High Performance Electronic Synapse

  • Developed and fabricated a bilayer memristor integrating Co-CeO2 VAN layer with a CeO2-based memristor to enhance device performance using PLD and sputtering.
  • Improved performance through selective conductive filament formation, achieving >67% reduction in set/reset voltage, increased reliability (endurance: >106 cycles, retention: >103 seconds), and better device-to-device variation (μ = 2.0 V, σ = 0.12 V, σ/μ = 0.06).
  • Conducted microstructural analysis (XRD, TEM, AFM) and electrical testing with a probe station and Keithley 4200A. Investigated the device switching mechanism using COMSOL and MATLAB.
  • Developed a spiking neural network (SNN) using the bilayer memristor in Python, achieving ~94% accuracy on the MNIST dataset after 12 iterations.
  • Supervised one graduate student in device fabrication techniques.
